Banana Chocolate Honey Puff Pastry

Image Source:halfbakedharvest

In my journey to eating healthier foods, I came across the realization that I have to stop learning to bake desserts. As much as you workout, it is impossible to overcome the calories consumed by a dessert madeup of all of your favourite sweet things.

This recipe is originally from Half Baked Harvest, but I add chopped walnuts to the recipe and prebake the bananas to ensure maximum sweetness. I could hardly get past 1/8 of it before I had to reconsider my dietary choices and packaged it up to give away.

The recipe is absolutely beyond delcious, but for your own health please make sure that you have someone to share with before you end up eating it all.

Prior to beginning, bake bananas at 400 for about 10 minutes to enhance their sweetness, even if already ripened.

Recipe List

  • 1 frozen puff pastry box, unfrozen
  • 200g milk chocolate, roughly chopped
  • 4 bananas
  • 4 tablespoons honey
  • 1 handfull of roasted walnuts, roughly chopped
  1. Preheat the oven to 425F
  2. Roll out the puff pasty into roughly the size of your baking sheet.
  3. Using a knife, score a crust around the edge of the puff pastry. Using a fork, poke holes into the center piece inside the crust to stop the inside from rising during baking.

    4. For the next few steps, assemble your puff pastry inside of the baking pan you plan to bake in to reduce any chance of mess.

  4. Spread chocolate over the entirety of the inside of the puff pastry, putting bananas on top.
  5. Sprinkle walnuts and honey over the chocolate and bananas, making sure to get some "accidentally" on the crust.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes, untill the edges are golden brown and the bananas have begun caramalizing.
